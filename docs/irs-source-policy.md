# IRS Source Policy

RefundIQ is designed to rely on authoritative IRS material first for internal research, estimate-supporting rule cards, and workflow alerts.

## Authority rules

- Tier 1: IRS forms, instructions, publications, tax professional guidance, MeF publications, IRIS A2A technical guidance, QuickAlerts
- Tier 2: Internal Revenue Manual and official state tax authority sources
- Tier 3: Internal SOPs and approved firm rule cards
- Tier 4: Secondary commentary for discovery only, never as final authority

## Non-negotiable boundaries

- RefundIQ does not claim that the IRS offers a universal refund-calculation API for this product
- RefundIQ does not represent itself as official IRS filing software
- Research answers must include citations for internal users
- Internal research answers should be generated from stored authoritative-source material first, with rule cards and source summaries treated as controlled internal synthesis layers
- Source conflicts must be surfaced and routed to human review
- Draft IRS material must stay marked as draft-only and cannot outrank final authoritative releases
- Client users must not see research citations, rule cards, or internal legal/tax analysis detail
